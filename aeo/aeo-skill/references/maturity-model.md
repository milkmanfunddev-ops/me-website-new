# The AEO Maturity Model

Source: Webflow, "AEO for the CMO: An Actionable Maturity Model"

Use this to assess where a user is and what to work on next. The model has four dimensions across five levels.

---

## Four Dimensions

1. **Content** — How answer-oriented is your content?
2. **Technical** — How well does your site structure support AI extraction?
3. **Authority** — How actively are you building citation-worthiness?
4. **Measurement** — How do you track AI visibility?

---

## Five Levels

### Level 1 — Keywords
Traditional keyword-focused content, basic on-page SEO, building backlinks, tracking keyword rankings. This is standard SEO with no AEO awareness.

### Level 2 — Answers
Starting to reorient around answers instead of just keywords. Creating some answer-oriented content, working on basic page structure, actively pursuing E-E-A-T principles, doing ad hoc mention checking. The transition from "rank for keywords" to "answer questions" is beginning.

### Level 3 — Structure
Getting systematic. Creating answer question clusters, building consistent site structure on a fast site, doing proactive digital PR and thought leadership, systematically tracking LLM traffic, mentions, and sentiment. AEO is becoming a deliberate part of the marketing strategy.

### Level 4 — Pillar
Optimizing for AEO as a thought leader. Creating answer-hierarchy-driven content, automated site structure, recognized as a pillar of thought leadership, tracking share of voice and accuracy. Content strategy is built around what AI systems need.

### Level 5 — Authority
Continuous adaptation to answer engine changes. Programmatic AEO with personalized content, adopted emerging standards (llms.txt, MCP), widespread positive citations, real-time analytics. AEO is fully integrated into the marketing operation.

---

## Self-Assessment Questions

Ask the user these to determine their level:

- **Content:** Do you target questions prospects ask as a primary way of choosing content topics?
- **Technical:** Does your website use schema.org structure widely?
- **Authority:** Does your team explicitly aim for Google's E-E-A-T?
- **Measurement:** Does your team measure mentions in LLMs?

Most companies are between Levels 1-3. That's fine. Progress beats perfection. Recommend focusing on getting to Level 3 solidly before trying to jump ahead.

---

## Recommended Actions by Level

### Moving from Level 1 to Level 2
- Rewrite top 5 page headings as questions users actually ask
- Add FAQ sections to product pages
- Start checking what AI says about your brand (manual audit)

### Moving from Level 2 to Level 3
- Implement the 5+3+3 query fan-out formula for key topics
- Add FAQPage, Article, and Organization schema
- Build Answer Blocks for top 10 customer questions
- Set up monthly AI visibility tracking spreadsheet
- Create llms.txt file

### Moving from Level 3 to Level 4
- Develop content clusters around answer hierarchies
- Pursue trade publication placements and expert contributor opportunities
- Implement automated schema across all content types
- Track share of AI voice vs. competitors
- Build entity optimization (Wikidata, Knowledge Graph)

### Moving from Level 4 to Level 5
- Adopt programmatic AEO at scale
- Implement real-time AI visibility monitoring tools
- Adopt emerging standards as they develop
- Integrate AI visibility metrics into marketing dashboards
